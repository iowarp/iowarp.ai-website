The latest from the IOWarp project — releases, grants, and milestones.

Dec 10, 2024
Grant

NSF Awards $5M for IOWarp Research

The National Science Foundation has awarded $5 million through Award #2411318 to fund IOWarp's development over 5 years (2024-2029). The grant supports research into context engineering infrastructure for autonomous AI agents in scientific computing.

PI: Dr. Xian-He Sun (Illinois Institute of Technology). Co-PIs: Dr. Anthony Kougkas (IIT), Dr. Kyle Chard (University of Utah). Partners: The HDF Group.

The funding will support development of the four core engines (CAE, CTE, CEI, PPI), deployment at national HPC centers, and training of next-generation researchers in context engineering.

Nov 14, 2024
Release

IOWarp Platform Beta Release

IOWarp v0.5-beta is now available for early adopters. This release includes the Content Transfer Engine (CTE) with MegaMmap-based intelligent tiering, initial Content Assimilation Engine (CAE) with HDF5 and NetCDF support, and Docker Compose deployment.

Performance highlights from SC'24 benchmarks: 20% faster than MPI-based tiered I/O, 2.6x DRAM reduction.

Get started: docker-compose up -d with the provided wrp_conf.yaml configuration.

Oct 15, 2024
Release

Agent Toolkit Release: 15+ MCP Servers

The IOWarp Agent Toolkit is now available with 15+ Model Context Protocol (MCP) servers providing 150+ tools for scientific computing. Servers include mcp-hdf5, mcp-netcdf, mcp-slurm, mcp-darshan, mcp-lmod, mcp-jarvis, mcp-spack, and more. Compatible with Claude Code, Cursor, and VS Code.

Install: claude mcp add iowarp-hdf5 -- npx @iowarp/mcp-hdf5

Sep 1, 2024
Team

Dr. Luke Logan Appointed Head Engineer

Dr. Luke Logan has been appointed as IOWarp's Head Engineer, bringing expertise in distributed systems, hierarchical storage, and HPC I/O optimization. Luke led the development of Hermes and LABIOS, the foundational technologies behind IOWarp's Content Transfer Engine.

His PhD research at Illinois Tech produced the MegaMmap framework presented at SC'24.

Aug 15, 2024
Press

Illinois Tech Features IOWarp Research

Illinois Institute of Technology's Gnosis Research Center has featured IOWarp as a flagship research project. The center's work on context engineering for autonomous AI agents represents a paradigm shift from traditional storage optimization to intelligent context orchestration.

The project combines 8 years of systems research with emerging AI agent capabilities.
